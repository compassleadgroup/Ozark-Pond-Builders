# Project Brief: Site 9, Ozark Pond Builders

The reference for what this site is and why it is built the way it is. Read this for context before the build. The execution detail lives in CLAUDE.md, COMPLIANCE.md, docs/content-data.md, and docs/seo-spec.md.

## Identity
- Site number: 9
- Brand: Ozark Pond Builders (confirm against the registered domain)
- Legal entity: Compass Camper LLC, doing business as Compass Lead Group
- Model: rank-and-rent. Rank in organic search, route landowner inquiries to one licensed local contractor for a fee. Matching service, not a contractor.
- Niche: pond and lake construction (farm and stock ponds, recreational lakes, pond dredging and renovation, dam construction and engineered repair)
- Geography: southwest Missouri county cluster

## Thesis
This niche satisfies the three near-mandatory criteria at once: a planned, high-consideration purchase that converts through organic browsing and a form fill, a high ticket, and a deep regulatory and soil content moat that competitors do not assemble. It also survives the number one killer, consolidator entry, with a structural gap: private equity has rolled up pond maintenance (recurring revenue) but not project-based pond construction (one-time earthmoving). Independent probability of success is roughly 62 percent.

## Market and geography
Target the rural county cluster, not Springfield city. Springfield city carries the most directory and rank-and-rent-shell competition and the highest map-pack presence. The ponds are dug, and the weak-web excavators operate, in the collar counties.
- Counties: Greene, Christian, Webster, Polk, Dallas
- Town pages: Marshfield, Rogersville, Ozark, Republic, Buffalo, Bolivar, Nixa
- Demand backdrop: Missouri has over 300,000 privately owned ponds and lakes (Missouri Department of Conservation). Demand is durable and rural, not tied to any expiring incentive.
- Expansion options if demand proves thin: Stone, Taney, and Lawrence counties, or a pivot of the same niche to the Tyler and East Texas market.

## Economics
- Ticket: a professionally built one-acre pond commonly starts around $45,000, a two-acre pond averages around $60,000, and acreage projects range roughly $30,000 to $150,000 with many near $90,000 (Bulletproof Pond and Lake). Sealing adds cost: clay sealant roughly $4,000 to $16,000 per acre, synthetic liner around $45,000 per acre (aquahabitat.com).
- Per-lead value: high, given the ticket. Realistic monetization is a hybrid of flat monthly and per-lead.
- Rent model: start flat at roughly $500 to $1,500 per month to one exclusive tenant once positions 1 to 3 are held, with a per-lead fallback of roughly $40 to $125 per qualified planned inquiry.
- Tenant profile: an owner-operator excavation or land-clearing company with 10 plus years of local work, a thin or no website, an equipment fleet, and capacity for $30,000 plus jobs.

## The moat
Each county and town page carries unique, citable regulatory and physical data that thin competitors lack: the federal Clean Water Act Section 404 trigger and the 404(f) farm-pond exemption, Missouri's 35-foot dam-safety threshold (Chapter 236 RSMo), NRCS Conservation Practice Standard 378, MU Extension watershed sizing (roughly 10 to 20 acres of drainage per surface acre of water), and county-level soil and water-holding reality from the NRCS Web Soil Survey. The Ozark cherty-soil angle (many local sites need a clay core or liner to hold water) is a genuine regional differentiator. This content triples as the ranking edge, the link bait, and the trust signal. Full data and sources are in docs/content-data.md.

## Search strategy in one paragraph
Organic only, no Google Business Profile. We capture planned buyers who scroll past the map pack to organic and submit a form over days or weeks. Money pages target transactional local terms (pond builder [town] MO, farm pond construction [county] county Missouri), which trigger AI Overviews only a small share of the time (roughly 5 to 8 percent on transactional and local intent) and remain reachable. Generic cost content is heavily cannibalized by AI Overviews, so it exists only as link bait and an AI-citation asset, never as a money page. Full keyword map and architecture in docs/seo-spec.md.

## Competitive landscape
- Organic number one for the core queries is a single, beatable local contractor (B & G Land Clearing, bglandclearing.com), which already builds town pages for Marshfield, Rogersville, Ozark, Republic, and Buffalo.
- National directories (BBB, YellowPages, SuperPages, HomeAdvisor, Porch, Thumbtack) appear but do not lock out local intent.
- A handful of likely thin rank-and-rent shells already target the area (low quality, beatable).
- Strong-site competitors are mostly out of area (Missouri Pond Company in St. Louis, Bulletproof Pond and Lake in Kansas).
- Consolidator screen: the pond roll-up is Jones Lake Management, a maintenance and management platform that grew from four to 26 states via 15 acquisitions and sold to Leonard Green and Partners on December 9, 2025. Construction is not the thesis of that roll-up. No identifiable PE platform targets pond construction earthmoving. Twenty-four-month entry probability into the construction segment in this metro is low.

## Targets and timeline
- Goal: 8 to 15 organic form-fill leads per month at organic positions 1 to 3 once ranked.
- Time to rank under free-only link building: roughly 5 to 9 months, faster on long-tail permit and dam queries, slower on the town and county money terms.
- Footprint: about 29 pages, held tight, each location page genuinely unique.

## Risks and pivot triggers
- Demand depth is the main unknown. The rural footprint may be thin. Pivot trigger: under roughly 150 combined monthly searches across the five counties on the money queries means expand the footprint to a second cluster or pivot the niche to Tyler and East Texas.
- Incumbent or shell hardens its SEO. Pivot trigger: failure to crack page one on three town queries by month six means push harder on the regulatory-content moat or move to the runner-up niche.
- A regional excavation roll-up emerges below press-release visibility. Action: monitor M&A quarterly and downgrade if a Missouri excavation platform is announced.
- Structural AI Mode and zero-click risk. The defense is exactly what current core updates reward: first-party content, proprietary local data, tight topical focus, a real brand, and a real service. Build those and measure beyond raw clicks.

## Two verifications the operator must run before promoting the site
1. A free Google Keyword Planner pull for exact monthly search volumes on the money queries across the five counties.
2. A manual incognito SERP check from a Springfield-area geolocation to confirm live map-pack, AI Overview, and ads rendering.
Build the site correctly in parallel. Confirm demand before promoting it to a paying tenant.
